Public Land

New York State owns $X billion in public land like parks and prisons.

The state pays its counties taxes on that land.
But not all counties.
Putnam and Rockland contain $1 billion in public lands.
The state pays them $30 million in town, county, and school taxes.
Nearby Westchester county contains $3.7 billion in public lands -- almost 4 times as much as its neighbors.
The state pays it $0 in taxes.
